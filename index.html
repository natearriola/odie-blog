LOVE AT FIRST LISTEN

BY NATE ARRIOLA

Nothing replaces the feeling of finding new music. It's a a blissful experience that everyone is familiar with and each experience is different depending on several factors, but in contrast, we share similar feelings of connection. A songs ability to connect with you is crucial. Whether that connection is superficial or intimate is irrelevant to the experience - the connection is still present, regardless of the level of impact it has on you. Recently, I've been missing this feeling - searching for music which teaches and enlightens me. Thus, my venture of finding new music began, and the discovery of ODIE.

ODIE first blessed my ears with his song Northface. Northface starts with a lo-fi aesthetic guitar and bass instruments. Both immediately grabbing my attention - mainly due to the vibey beat. However, the production wasn't the reason I kept listening - his angelic vocals are what stops me from hitting next. It comes off so effortlessly and passion filled, leaving you in awe wondering how one dude can have so much talent. A raw approach to singing is a breath of fresh air in the world of auto-tune and heavy vocal processing. Furthermore, lyrically ODIE's storytelling of experiences is creative and relatable. Telling through familiar themes such as hope, religion, ambition, goals, finding a soul mate, etc. Such things we've experienced or encountered at least once in our lives. Now, these themes can be found in every song but how ODIE presents them fills you with a different perspective. His lyrics aren't hallowed or valueless the words have an impact and stick to you like white on rice. Simply, the kid has it all. Everything that I've been wanting from music. But most importantly his authenticity and unique sound pave the way of connecting with his listeners. And that's all I've ever wanted. A connection.
